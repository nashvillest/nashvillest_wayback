Bright & Early: Gloomy Weather For A Gloomy Day Edition

March 12th, 2009 · Comments

Good morning, Nashville. Yesterday, the State of Tennessee received a special and kind-of-sad delivery from Miami containing the giant American flag that flew over Ground Zero after 9/11. Since it first left New York, the flag has been making the rounds all over the world to honor soldiers who have been killed in the war. And this time it’s our turn after a Tennessee National Guardsman and a lieutenant from the Sequatchie County Sheriff’s Office were killed in Iraq last month. If you’re downtown today, you can catch the flag flying over the Tennessee State Capitol.

  • New jobs. You know it’s bad when even the minimum-wage summer jobs aren’t hiring. Luckily we may be in line for 12,000 summer jobs for low-income teens, except they’ll be at state parks and government offices which sounds like a boring way to spend the summer. But hey, it pays the bills.
  • Abortion bill advances. A controversial bill limiting abortion access passed in the Tennessee Senate yesterday. Even if it passes this year, it would also have to pass a few more rounds before being placed on the ballot in 2014.
  • Bits & pieces. In case you haven’t been outside today, it’s super-nasty … A Dickson County man stole a school bus to tow his real car out of the ditch … State legislators are finding new ways to try and loosen gun regulations … When everyone on the planet gets laid off, they apparently go out and buy suits.
