Citywide Snowball Fight: Tonight At 6PM, Centennial Park [#ArcticBlast2011]

10 January 2011 | 2:45 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

As we’re sure you know by now, it snowed last night in Nashville. And snow can only mean one thing, other than the bread & milk shelves emptying out: It’s time for a citywide snowball fight! (Okay, so snow can mean a lot of things, especially in Nashville, but we’re prioritizing.)

We attended an impromptu snowball fight last year in Centennial Park, and magical things happened. A mythical beer truck may or may not have provided belly-warming nourishment. We ambushed news crews with snowballs. We slid on cookie sheets pulled by bicycles. We pub-crawled afterward. We made a bunch of new friends. At a citywide snowball fight, all your wildest dreams can come true.

So! Join us tonight at 6pm-ish in Centennial Park for the first citywide snowball fight of 2011, if you can get out of your driveways. If you’re for real snowed in, start throwing snowballs at your neighbors and see if you can’t get some fun started of your very own!

Citywide Snowball Fight. Monday, January 10, 2011. Centennial Park (West End side of the Parthenon). Bring sleds, cookie sheets, and bundle up!

“The Leg Has Got To Go” [Gems From The Nashville Craigslist]

10 January 2011 | 11:30 am
Author: Morgan Levy

…And you thought fruitcake was bad. Bringing a whole new meaning to re-gifting, our first Gem from the Nashville Craigslist of 2011:

Female Mannequin Leg (East Nashville)

Ok, y’all. My friend went to a Christmas party and received this mannequin leg as a gag gift. It was pretty funny for a while, but my roommate (his lady) and I told him from the start that it was not welcome in the house. Of course that meant he brought it in the house. It has terrorized various rooms, but tonight was the last straw. He apparently decided it would be fun to hide it in a corner on the front porch. I went to walk my dog, saw it out of the corner of my eye, and nearly had a heart attack, thinking there was a person on my porch in the ghetto.

THE LEG HAS GOT TO GO. Seriously. I don’t know what you’d do with it. We’ve had some ideas…turn it into a lamp of sorts, use it as a coat tree, make an art project…the possibilities are endless, really. The leg is nicely shaped, as you can see from the pictures below. It is well toned and has a small, but firm, derriere.

Please come get this leg. Before I beat my friend with it. It is free to a good home. It just wants to be loved.

Bright & Early: Snow On The Brain Edition

10 January 2011 | 10:00 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. You might have noticed that’s snowing outside! Most of Nashville received anywhere from 2-4″ last night, resulting in school closures, canceled flights, hazardous driving conditions and an official state of emergency. According to weather forecasts, we’ve probably got another inch or two on the way tonight into tomorrow. We can has snow day?

  • Inauguration time. Governator-to-be Bill Haslam kicks off his inauguration festivities this week with a tour around the state leading up to his inauguration ceremony on Friday night.
  • Less green, more roof. Developers had to slash part of the green roof plans for the Music City Center in order to stay within budget. With the new plan, greenery will cover about 28% of the roof’s surface.
  • Bits & pieces. MTA buses will be running on snow routes today and probably also tomorrow … NES suspends energy disconnections for folks who can’t pay their bills when temperatures drop below freezing … Bud Adams really wanted to keep Vince Young and Coach Fisher, but “couldn’t find a way to make the relationship work” … Fort Campbell’s 101st Air Division has had its deadliest combat year since Vietnam … A fire destroyed Charlie Daniels’ barn over the weekend and killed 11 horses … Former Congressman Zach Wamp’s father has died after falling at his office in Chattanooga.
