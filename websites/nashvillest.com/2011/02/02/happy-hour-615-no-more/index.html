Happy Hour: 615 No More

2 February 2011 | 6:30 pm
Author: Christy Frink

  • Nashville’s running out of 615 area codes! By summer 2013, there will probably be a new area code for folks moving to the area. [WSMV]
  • The Tennessee Grocers and Convenience Store Association estimate that allowing wine to be sold in grocery stores like normal states could create as many as 3,000 jobs for Tennesseans. [City Paper]
  • TVA customers–ourselves included–are still paying an average of 69 cents per month on the cleanup of the December 2008 Kingston Ash Spill. [WZTV]
  • Earlier today, the headline for this story read “Doctor Testifies About Rectal Search in Crack Case.” It has since been changed to something far less interesting. [WKRN]
  • The Tennessee Bureau of Investigation is fully on board with legislation that would require a prescription for cold medicines and other types of over-the-counter drugs that can be used to cook meth. [WTVF]
  • More than 100 people were expected to show up to peacefully protest Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak by the Nashville Farmer’s Market late this afternoon. [WSMV]
  • The state is working hard to put an end to the latest drug fad before it even starts in Tennessee: “the abuse of so-called bath salts.” So-called!

  • Craig

    The reason I am guessing the second code will be 546 is because when you look at the list of numbers that are listed as unassignable (the list is at http://bellsmind.net/BellsMind/NPA?NPA=615 if you want to see it), that is the only set that does not match an existing area code or is kept for special use or testing. (What I mean is that there are no 615-546-xxxx numbers).

    It would definitely be preferable for an “overlay” to take place, meaning that once 615 numbers run out, they will start assigning new (546?) numbers throughout the Nashville area. That avoids a split which would be extremely unpopular in the outlying suburbs (they would lose 615 and from my knowledge, the relations between Nashville and its outlying suburbs are among the best of any city in the US so that association being lost would be devastating for them) and these days are extremely challenging technically. Recently, in Pennsylvania, there was a highly heated battle over an extremely unpopular split (see http://www.save814.com) and their authority has been forced to review the decision.

    The reason for 10-digit dialing would be because the same sets of numbers (i.e. 555-6789) would exist in both 615 and (546?), and they wouldn’t know how to route them since they could both be Nashville numbers.
