Happy Hour: You Go Girls

18 February 2011 | 5:30 pm
Author: Christy Frink

  • A recent survey found that Tennessee has “at least one female CEO” these days, and she leads Regal Entertainment in Knoxville. Less than 10% of Tennessee’s corporate board positions are held by women. [WPLN]
  • A temporary free medical clinic will be set up at McGavock High School this weekend, staffed by hundreds of awesome volunteers. [WKRN]
  • Metro is still trying to figure up how to make up all those snow days, so they’re surveying parents to help determine the best option. The current options are listed out on MNPS’ website. [WSMV]
  • Mayor Dean has once again voiced his support for the proposed nondiscrimination ordinance, which would require all Metro contractors with more than 15 employees to institute a nondiscrimination policy that covers sexual orientation and gender identity. [WPLN]
  • Interim TSU president Portia Holmes Shields is making a ton of changes at the university to hopefully help get things back on track. While we’re not sure that requiring staff to answer the phones with, “Good morning, TSU, the land of golden sunshine” will do a whole lot to help, it sure does make us want to call them. [WSMV]

The Weekendist: This Little Piggy Went To Market Edition

18 February 2011 | 12:00 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

TGIF, Nashville. We know, we know, we talk about this weather a lot, but… Isn’t it amazing? Enjoy a weekend free of slush and ice (unless it’s in the form of a frozen treat) and if you’re looking for something fun to do, our top picks are below.

FRIDAY

  • Night Market. Nashville Farmer’s Market, 6-8pm, free admission (just pay for the goodies!). We’ve raved about how much we love the idea of Night Market before, but January’s event was the first we had the good fortune of actually attending. It didn’t disappoint. A packed house at the farmer’s market grazed tables with local cheese samples, boozy hot chocolate (and homemade marshmallows) and ate their fill of BBQ, gyros and naan. This month’s Night Market will feature more of the same, plus the return of Fleur de Lis Flavors, The Green Bag Lady and more! Tip: Bring cash.

SATURDAY

  • Over The Rhine Instore. Grimey’s, 5pm, free. Before they take the stage at 3rd & Lindsley on Sunday night, Over the Rhine will be playing an instore at Grimey’s this Saturday. The instore is free, but the Girl Scouts will also be there peddling their delicious cookies. Basically, you need to prepare for a wallop in the wallet (who can resist those Girl Scouts and their cookies? Not us).
  • Vishnu: Hinduism’s Blue-Skinned Savior at the Frist. Frist Center, 10am-5:30pm, free. Saturday marks the last day of the almost month-long run of free admission at the Frist, so if you haven’t taken advantage of it yet, what are you waiting for?! Saturday also includes a free community preview of the Frist’s new exhibition, Vishnu: Hinduism’s Blue-Skinned Savior. Hear the curator’s perspective on the exhibit at 11am, and watch an accompanying dance performance at 1pm.
  • 104-5 The Zone’s SportsFest. Nashville Convention Center, 8am-5pm, $5. From NASCAR to football to hockey, there’s something for every sports fan at Sportsfest. Speakers include NASCAR driver Sterling Marlin, UT Head Football Coach Derek Dooley, brand-new head coach of the Tennessee Titans Mike Munchak and more.
  • Nashville Predators vs. Phoenix Coyotes. Bridgestone Arena, 7pm, ticket prices vary. Cheer on the Preds as they face the Coyotes for the final time this season. We’ve only got a couple months left of hockey season, so be sure to catch a game or two before it’s over!

SUNDAY

  • Swing Dance at the Farmer’s Market. Nashville Farmer’s Market, 1-4pm, free. It’s quite the busy weekend at the Nashville Farmer’s Market. Lace up your shoes and get your swing dancing on inside the Market House this Sunday afternoon.

Bright & Early: This Is Our Friday Face Edition

18 February 2011 | 9:30 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. In the past year, local law enforcement reports that home burglaries have increased 18-20% all over Nashville. Though police haven’t quite identified a reason for that, Nashvillians are being urged to take more precautions against break-ins like getting an alarm system, joining a local Neighborhood Watch, getting to know our neighbors and making sure we’ve got insurance to cover our losses if someone steals our stuff.

  • Sales tax increase for Williamson? Already in a state with one of the highest sales taxes in the country, Williamson County may soon raise their sales tax by a half-percent. County officials say that they’ve trimmed the budget as much as they can and just need some more revenue.
  • Chicken biscuits 4eva. Chick-Fil-A is scoping out a vacant bank at the corner of Harding and Woodlawn in Belle Meade for a potential new drive-thru location. The problem is that current zoning policies forbid drive-thrus on corner lots, so some changes would have to be made that could take a while. For now, we’ll just have to keep driving all the way to Brentwood or braving the Batman Building.
  • Bits & pieces. The movie “Deadline” starring Eric Roberts and Steven Talley is scheduled to be filmed at Rippavilla Plantation in Spring Hill, home of the best corn maze ever … In addition to being the fourth brainiest city, Nashville was ranked the 19th best shopping city in the US by Forbes … Governor Haslam is proposing a new law that would increase the tenure requirements for public school teachers … Metro Finance Director Rich Riebeling has fixed his ethics disclosure forms, thank you very much.

Rites Of Spring 2011: Kid Cudi, The National, Public Enemy, Sara Bareilles Top The Bill

18 February 2011 | 2:32 am
Author: Morgan Levy

As always, a few days after the big Bonnaroo announcement we get another whopper of a lineup announcement courtesy of the folks at Rites of Spring, Vanderbilt’s spring mini-fest (happening this year April 15-16). The only problem with Vandy’s lineup announcements is that they’re really, really, really bad at keeping them under wraps. Last year, their student site InsideVandy leaked the info early, and with that in mind, we weren’t shocked when a full week before performers were supposed to be announced, the Rites of Spring site started showing this year’s lineup a few days ago.

…Or was it? They quickly changed the performers on the site, claiming it was just a test, but that didn’t stop organizers from hustling out an official announcement a few days ahead of schedule (and at midnight. Seriously guys, bloggers gotta sleep sometime). And what do you know! It looks like that “test site” was not so much of a test. Kid Cudi, The National, Public Enemy and Sara Bareilles will top the Rites of Spring 2011 bill, joined by Edward Sharpe & The Magnetic Zeros and Matt & Kim. Tickets go on sale next Thursday at Ticketmaster or the Sarratt Student Center box office.

Rites of Spring 2011. April 15-16 at Vanderbilt University. Tickets on sale February 24. Student tickets $30, single day tickets $45, weekend pass $55 in advance, $65 day-of.
