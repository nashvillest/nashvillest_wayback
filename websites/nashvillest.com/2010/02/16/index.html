Happy Hour: It’s 4:20 Somewhere

16 February 2010 | 5:30 pm
Author: Christy Frink

  • A new bill is being introduced in the Tennessee legislature that would legalize medical marijuana for people with certain medical conditions for $60 per ounce. [WKRN]
  • In the wake of announcing more than 1,000 layoffs in the state government, Governor Phil says he considered shutting state offices down every other Friday and furloughing employees instead. [WTVF]
  • The TVA is still maintaining that their ash spill cleanup in Kingston is going well and is not posing a safety risk to anyone or anything. Something about the words “potentially toxic” and “laced with arsenic” does not compute in our minds, but we’ll take their word for it. [WTVF]
  • Thanks to all the winter weather, TDOT is having to stretch their budget by cutting back on things like picking up litter and mowing medians, which should make for some beautiful highways in the meantime. [WSMV]
  • The Davidson County Sheriff’s Department has a $10,000 shiny new toy that will help them to better track sex offenders using iris scan technology. [WTVF]
  • Tennessee is not doing so well. [WSMV]

Bright & Early: Enough Already Edition

16 February 2010 | 9:30 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. If you’re looking out the window this morning and wondering how the heck it’s still snowing, we’re right there with you. And if you didn’t know it was snowing, a quick skim of the headlines would probably tip you off. We’re seeing everything from kids going without food because school is closed to snow driving down the price of roses to the Nashville Shores expansion soldiering on through the snow to cute animals playing in the snow at the Nashville Zoo to El Niño bringing us even more snow in March to Metro Schools wondering how they’re going to make up all of these snow days. Tired of snow yet, Nashville?

  • Nashville firefighters battling cancer. Due to recent health data indicating that a shocking 8.6% of Nashville’s firefighters are suffering from cancer, researchers believe that the numbers may be linked to exposure to diesel fumes from fire engines idling in closed spaces. Nearly 70 Nashville firefighters currently have cancer and 30 others have died from cancer in the past 20 years.
  • Love, true love. Over the weekend, a local couple got married in a Murfreesboro White Castle where one of them had worked for four years. According to the (very short) article, her “White Castle family” was also able to attend the wedding. Best of luck, you crazy lovebirds.
  • Bits & pieces. Those with keyless remote starters may soon be able to warm up their cars without risking a ticket … Vanderbilt University is being nationally recognized for their innovative use of social media … Tennessee will kick off a new Cash for Appliances program on April 22 to help homeowners replace non-energy efficient appliances in their homes using funds from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act … Councilman Eric Crafton will defer his fairgrounds preservation bill to give the council more time to come up with a plan to save the fairgrounds … Domestic assaults were up on Valentine’s Day, including one woman who went crazy on her boyfriend when he had flowers delivered to her.
