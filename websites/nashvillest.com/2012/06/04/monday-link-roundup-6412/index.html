Monday Link Roundup: 6/4/12

June 4, 2012 1:00 PM 2 comments

Share this Article

  • Facebook
  • Delicious

  • Reddit

Author:

Morgan Levy
  • Word broke late last week that Nashville-based Gaylord will sell its hotel brand to Marriott for a cool $210 million.
  • Nashville is bracing for CMApocalypse to descend upon the city. Bingo coming soon, we promise!
  • The water in Franklin stinks again, quite literally.
  • Hickory Hollow mall will officially close this summer, with Metro planning for a rec center, library and public “green space” at the location sometime in the future.
  • Traffic was really really bad this weekend due to road and bridge closures, which you already know if you tried to go anywhere near I-24.
  • VOTE 4 PEKKA!
  • Nine new charter schools will open in Memphis and Nashville next year, serving 3,000 students.
  • Cool Springs Galleria is getting a real, live Apple Store on the heels of MacAuthority’s departure from the mall.
  • Bits & pieces: Frothy Monkey is opening a new location in downtown Franklin … TDOT is now working on safety improvements to a Vietnam Veterans Blvd bridge after a 100-car pileup in December … We love Robyn and we love that this video of two Leiper’s Fork sisters covering Robyn has gone viral … More than 2,000 job hunters showed up at Saturday’s Nissan job fair … A new gourmet burger joint, Burger Republic, has opened in Lenox Village … Gas prices are down, and we all rejoice.
