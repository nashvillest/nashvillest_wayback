Win, Win, Win: Nashville Loves Contests

22 August 2008 | 7:00 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

There are probably 5 Nashville-centric contests going on at any given point in time. Right now we’ve got Capture Music City, Music City Madness, MTA Jingle Contest, Music City Songwriting Contest and The Flip Side of Nashville contest. Some contests are unique, some are overdone, and some are a miserable failure (some are a combination of these). We just love the contests that require videos and let us view them as they roll in. Thank you, Flip Side of Nashville contest, for this afternoon’s entertainment. 

Incidentally, if any of you Nashvillians out there are in need of a trip to Nashville (the grand prize), get to work. There’s only 8 videos in the group right now, and we have a feeling that about half of them don’t count as legitimate entries. The contest deadline is August 25 (er, looks like they extended it to September 1… wonder why), and they’re doling out over 1,000 prizes. The odds of winning are in your favor.

12 South Concert Series Starts This Weekend

22 August 2008 | 3:11 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

As if we didn’t have enough to do this Saturday (plans include the Dragon Boat races, a trip to the Farmer’s Market to visit The Sugar Bar and a little exploration of the new DrinkHaus in Germantown), another addition to the ever-growing pile of outdoor concert series is begging to be added to our calendar. The 12 South Neighborhood Association is introducing 12 South Concerts this weekend – a family (and pet) friendly concert series happening every other Saturday from now through October 4. It’s a perfect excuse to get outside and enjoy the end of summer at Sevier Park with free music (5pm-9pm each night).

Bright & Early: Booze It And Lose It Edition

22 August 2008 | 9:37 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. According to Metro police, one in four clerks in Middle Tennessee will sell alcohol to underage kids. In an effort to get those numbers down, they’ve been visiting stores all over the region to test vendors by sending a 19-year old inside to try to purchase some booze. Those who break the law are ticketed and fined up to $1500 for their first offense, along with the possibility of the store’s license to sell alcohol being taken away.

  • Showing who’s Boss. It seems like we’re the only ones who weren’t at the Bruce Springsteen concert last night, but at least we can read about it.
  • Recycle, reduce, reuse? Copies of driver’s licenses and social security cards were found unshredded at the Green Hills recycling center behind Hillsboro High School. No one knows where they came from yet, but Public Works suspects it’s a check-cashing/payday loan service.
  • Bits & pieces. Brace yourselves for the biggest increase in food prices in 20 years … MTA is in the process of taking the reins of the Music City Star from RTA in hopes of making it not suck … Members of the Kurdish Pride gang have been targeting individual police officers in spray-painted threats around the city … The Wilson County fair has already surpassed last year’s attendance by 60,000 and still runs through tomorrow.
