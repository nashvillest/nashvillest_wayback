This Weekend: Dragon Boat & River Festival

20 August 2008 | 12:36 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

There aren’t many things that can drag us out of Nashvillest HQ at 9am on Saturday, but we will make the sacrifice for something that’s really worth it. Dragon Boat & River Festival, please don’t let us down. This weekend will see teams of 22 people racing the distinctive long, skinny dragon boats down the Cumberland. While it’s no flugtag, the Dragon Boat Races are bound to be fun to watch. The opening ceremonies begin at Riverfront Park at 8:30am and the first race starts at 9am, although they continue throughout the day until the 3:45pm Grand Championship Finale.

Bright & Early: Going Batty Edition

20 August 2008 | 9:21 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. We knew the Sounds were having a bad season, but we didn’t realize it was on track to be the worst one in franchise history. With just 14 games left in the season, they’re going to have to pull off 10 wins if they want to dodge that milestone. We’ve always been glass-half-full kind of people, but we’re having a hard time being optimistic about this one. Nashvillest wishes them the best in the final stretch, though.

  • Don’t tase me, bro! Metro police are rolling out a new kind of Taser that will have a camera on it to add more accountability to the weapon. And WSMV would like to remind you that there could be 120 of them on the streets at any given time.
  • Georgia loses. It turns out that the Georgians didn’t really find Bigfoot after all and were just making stuff up. It was actually just a rubber gorilla suit frozen in a block of ice. Nice try, guys, but you still can’t have our water.
  • Bits & pieces. Metro schools are finally starting to publicize the option for parents to send their kids to other schools due to Metro’s failure to meet the No Child Left Behind standards … Councilman Crafton withdrew another controversial measure that would have imposed a special fee for immigrants to use government services, but plans to refile … New developments keep rolling in about what’s now being called “Bredesen’s Watergate.“
