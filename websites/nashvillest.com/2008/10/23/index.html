Twitter Feed: Best Of Both Worlds

23 October 2008 | 8:55 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

Thanks to everyone who gave us feedback in our Twitter survey earlier this afternoon. Most of you wanted to keep seeing our posts in your Twitter timeline, so we’ve created @nashvillestRSS for all of you. We have turned off the feed on @nashvillest, but we’ll continue to use that account to chat, throw out tips, wish you happy birthday, and update you on our whereabouts as we tramp around Nashville poking our noses where they don’t belong. In other words, we’ll be using @nashvillest in the manner that Twitter was intended. Please follow one, both or neither of the accounts… whatever floats your boat!

We hope that this decision will make (almost) everyone happy! As always, if you think we should be doing something differently, leave us a comment or email us. We’re here for Nashville, and we aren’t doing our job if our readers aren’t happy. 

For those not on Twitter… Sorry about the irrelevant posts. But now, we’ll get back to our regularly scheduled programming.

This Weekend: Great Nashville Duck Race

23 October 2008 | 12:31 pm
Author: Morgan Levy

There’s nothing better than watching thousands of rubber ducks getting dumped into the Cumberland River and then “racing” for prizes, right? We love the Great Nashville Duck Race because it’s outrageously silly to watch, everyone can participate and it’s for a good cause. Just adopt a rubber duck for $5 and the proceeds go to The Boys & Girls Club of Middle Tennessee. And you have a shot at winning the rubber duck race… and even if you don’t win, you’ll get your $5 worth of entertainment as you watch 7,500 rubber ducks race down the riverfront. Really, we promise it’s worth it. Come out this Saturday to check out the fun! 

  • 11:00am – Family Fun Festivities & Nashville Predators Kids Zone at Riverfront Park and Taco Bell V.I.D. Party at Riverfront Landing
  • 1:00pm – Race Begins at Gateway Bridge
  • 1:30pm - Race Ends at Riverfront Park
  • 2:00pm - Announcement of Winning Lucky Ducks at Riverfront Park
We’re assuming that V.I.D. stands for “Very Important Duck” and can’t wait to see what THAT’S all about.

Bright & Early: Lights Out Edition

23 October 2008 | 9:15 am
Author: Christy Frink

Good morning, Nashville. Businesses downtown are resorting to some pretty drastic measures in order to save money and electricity: They’re actually turning off the lights when they leave at night. In the name of good stewardship (and saving hundreds of thousands of dollars), some are even installing timers to shut off the lights at 9pm–so we hope nobody’s planning on working late. The real extremists are setting computers to hibernate after a few minutes of idleness. We’re glad you’re doing your part, Nashville.

  • Roma suspects charged. The two guys who allegedly robbed Roma Pizza and murdered one employee are in court today and have both been charged with first degree murder and robbery.
  • Thanksgiving travel hell. Book your flights early this year because BNA will be seeing about 2,000 less planes leaving the tarmac this Thanksgiving compared to last year, which is a little more than a 10% reduction. Flights are being cut all over the country due to (what else?) the rising cost of fuel. But we’re still holding out hope for Southwest who’s actually adding flights to meet the high demand because they are all kinds of awesome. We’re just not sure if any of them are passing through of Nashville.
  • Bits & pieces. Our voting machines are the same kind of messed-up as West Virginia’s … The Metro Planning Commission is setting up to vote on what could be the biggest Habitat for Humanity development in the country if it passes … Governor Phil is off schmoozing his way around Germany trying to score us some new auto supplier jobs.
